About ComplaintCA

Last updated: July 21, 2026

Most people don't lose a fight with an institution because they were wrong. They lose it because they didn't know which door to knock on, missed a deadline no one told them about, or gave up navigating a system built for lawyers, not people. ComplaintCA exists to fix that first, hardest step.

Art. IMission

Route every complaint to the right door

ComplaintCA identifies the correct Canadian authority for a complaint — municipal, provincial, or federal — and helps a person file it correctly, the first time. It offers AI-assisted drafting in 7 languages, an MP finder, cyber crime reporting, access-to-information requests, and a panel that connects legal-track cases with independent partner lawyers.

Art. IIVision

No one turned away for not knowing the process

A Canada where filing a complaint against a landlord, an employer, or a government office doesn't require a law degree, a paid consultation, or hours lost to the wrong office. Where the deciding factor in whether someone is heard is whether they were wronged — not whether they knew how the system works.

Art. IIIHow It Works

Three paths, one platform

Notify an institution — put a formal complaint on record, with the deadline tracked. Assert a legal right — build a case file organized well enough that an independent partner lawyer can pick it up and act. Request information — file the access-to-information request a government body is legally required to answer.

Art. IVVerification

Real authorities, never AI guesses

Every authority, deadline, and legal citation shown to a user is drawn from a manually verified reference table — not generated freely by the AI. If a detail isn't confirmed, the system says so rather than invent one. Every guide links directly to the official page it references, so nothing has to be taken on trust.

Art. V — Independence

ComplaintCA is a civil society platform. Advertising and sponsorship are not for sale here — no offer of either, from any government, political party, or corporation, will be accepted.

It carries no political affiliation and receives no political support, of any kind, from any side. This is an independent human rights effort, standing on its own — not a cause anyone gets to attach their name to.

Art. VIWhat We Are Not
  • Not a law firm. ComplaintCA does not provide legal advice or representation.
  • Not a finished document. AI-drafted content is a starting point to review, not a filed legal submission.
  • Not the decision-maker. Every outcome is decided by the actual authority the complaint is filed with.
