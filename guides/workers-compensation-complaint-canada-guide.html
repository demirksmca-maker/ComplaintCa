Appealing a Denied Workers' Compensation Claim

A workplace injury claim denied, or benefits cut off before you're ready to return to work? Every province runs its own workers' compensation board, but they all follow a similar two-level appeal pattern — with deadlines that are genuinely strict.

Be aware: Every province's board has its own name, forms, and deadlines. This guide covers the two largest boards (Ontario's WSIB and BC's WorkSafeBC) in detail and the general pattern elsewhere — always confirm your own province's specific deadline before you wait.

Ontario — WSIB

1File an Intent to Object

If you disagree with a WSIB decision, file an Intent to Object. WSIB will review and may confirm, amend, or revoke it — this results in a final decision from an Appeals Resolution Officer (ARO).

2Appeal to WSIAT (independent, final)

If the ARO decision is still unfavourable, appeal to the Workplace Safety and Insurance Appeals Tribunal — separate from and independent of WSIB. You generally have 6 months from the final WSIB decision to file the Notice of Appeal.

British Columbia — WorkSafeBC

1Review Division

The first level of review for most WorkSafeBC decisions.

2WCAT (final)

The Workers' Compensation Appeal Tribunal is the second and final level. File the Notice of Appeal before the deadline.

Other Provinces

The general pattern holds everywhere

Alberta, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Quebec (CNESST), and the Atlantic provinces each run their own board with an internal review followed by an independent external appeal tribunal. Deadlines are typically 6-12 months but vary — confirm yours directly with your board.

Frequently Asked

My WSIB claim was denied — what do I do?

File an Intent to Object with WSIB, then appeal to WSIAT within 6 months of the final decision if still denied.

How does the process work in BC?

WorkSafeBC's Review Division first, then WCAT as the final level.

Is it the same everywhere?

The general pattern is similar, but names, forms, and deadlines differ by province — always confirm yours directly.
