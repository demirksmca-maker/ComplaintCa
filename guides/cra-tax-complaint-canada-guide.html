CRA Problems: Two Different Processes

A bad experience with a CRA agent, and disagreeing with a tax bill, are not the same thing — they go through completely different processes with different deadlines. Getting this right matters, because one of them can permanently close your rights if you miss the window.

Service complaint

How CRA treated you

Rude staff, unreasonable delays, unclear information, or a process CRA didn't follow properly. This goes to the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson.

Assessment dispute

What CRA says you owe

You disagree with the amount on a Notice of (Re)assessment. This goes through a formal Notice of Objection — strict deadlines apply.

If It's About Service

Step 1 — CRA Service Feedback Program

Raise it with the CRA directly first — call the number on your correspondence, or ask to speak with a supervisor.

Step 2 — Taxpayers' Ombudsperson

If it's still unresolved, or you're unsatisfied with CRA's response, escalate here. This office reviews service quality only — not whether an assessment is correct.

Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson — 1-866-586-3839 →

If It's About an Assessment

File a Notice of Objection

Generally due within 90 days of the date on your Notice of (Re)assessment — for individuals, whichever is later of that or one year after your filing deadline. Missing it can permanently end your right to object, though a late-filing request exists within a further limited window.

Frequently Asked

I had a bad experience with a CRA agent — where do I complain?

Start with the CRA's Service Feedback Program, then the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson at 1-866-586-3839 if unresolved.

Is disputing my assessment the same process?

No — that's a Notice of Objection, a completely different, deadline-driven process.

How long do I have to object?

Generally 90 days from the notice date, or for individuals, one year after your filing deadline — whichever is later.

What is ComplaintCA?

A Canadian civic complaint platform that routes you to the correct authority, with AI-assisted drafting in 7 languages, an MP finder, a lawyer panel, and cyber crime reporting support.

I'm Deaf or hard of hearing — how do I call a phone-only authority?

Use Canada VRS (free sign-language video relay, available 24/7) or dial 711 for a text-based relay operator — both let you place a real phone call to any number listed on this page.
