Report In This Order

1. If money or banking details were already shared

Contact your bank or credit card issuer immediately — ask about reversing the transaction, freezing the card, or flagging the account. Minutes matter here.

2. Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre (CAFC)

The national body for tracking fraud and cyber crime intelligence across Canada. It does not investigate individual cases, but reporting is essential for police and regulators to act on patterns.

Report at antifraudcentre-centreantifraude.ca — 1-888-495-8501 →

Note: the CAFC does not accept reports by email — use the phone line or its online Fraud Reporting System.

3. Your local police service

File a report with your local police (non-emergency line) — this creates an official record, which some banks and insurers require to process a claim.

4. Verify before you act — Asya's document check

Not sure if a message, invoice, or email is really a scam? ComplaintCA's verification tool analyzes an uploaded screenshot or document for signs of phishing or fraud, entirely on your device before anything is reported.

Common Cyber Crime Types

  • Phishing — fake emails/texts impersonating banks, government, or delivery services asking for credentials or payment.
  • Identity theft — someone using your personal information to open accounts or make purchases.
  • Romance & investment scams — long-term manipulation building trust before requesting money or crypto.
  • Online harassment & extortion — threats, doxxing, or sextortion attempts.

Frequently Asked

Where do I report cyber crime or online fraud in Canada?

The Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre at 1-888-495-8501 or reportcyberandfraud.canada.ca, and your local police service.

Does the CAFC accept reports by email?

No — use the phone line or the online Fraud Reporting System only.

How can I check if a message is a phishing scam?

Upload it to ComplaintCA's document verification tool for an AI analysis, done on your device.

I already sent money — what do I do first?

Contact your bank or card issuer immediately, then report to the CAFC and your local police.

What is ComplaintCA?

A Canadian civic complaint platform that routes you to the correct authority, with AI-assisted drafting in 7 languages, an MP finder, a lawyer panel, and cyber crime reporting support.

I'm Deaf or hard of hearing — how do I call a phone-only authority?

Use Canada VRS (free sign-language video relay, available 24/7) or dial 711 for a text-based relay operator — both let you place a real phone call to any number listed on this page.
