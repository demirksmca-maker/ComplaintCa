How to File a Complaint Against Canada Post

Lost package, damaged delivery, or a customer service issue Canada Post won't fix? There's a required first step before the Ombudsman — Canada's post office runs its own final appeal authority, and it only steps in once your ticket with Canada Post itself is closed.

1Check the basics first

Confirm the expected delivery date has passed, double-check the address was correct, and check Canada Post's service alerts for known delays in your area.

2Open a support ticket

If the package still can't be found or the issue isn't resolved, ask Canada Post to start an investigation by opening a support ticket.

Canada Post Support →

3Appeal to the Ombudsman (final authority)

Once your ticket is closed and you're not satisfied, appeal to the Office of the Ombudsman with your documentation (receipts, ticket number, correspondence). Expect a follow-up within 5-10 business days.

Frequently Asked

Can I appeal to the Ombudsman right away?

No — you need a closed service ticket from Canada Post customer service first.

What should I check before contacting anyone?

The expected delivery date, your address accuracy, and Canada Post's service alerts.

How long does the Ombudsman take?

They aim to follow up within 5-10 business days.
