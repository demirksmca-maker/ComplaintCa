How to File a Complaint Against a Landlord in Canada

Last updated: July 21, 2026 · Reviewed against official sources

Tenancy law in Canada is provincial, so the right place to file depends on where you live. This guide lists the tenancy board for each major province — and shows where ComplaintCA fits in to help you draft the complaint, in your own language.

Lease disputes, repairs, and eviction matters for Quebec tenants and landlords.

Other provinces & territories

Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI, Newfoundland and Labrador, and the territories each have their own residential tenancy office. Search "[your province] residential tenancy office" or ask Asya below — it can point you to the right one.

Before You File

  • Keep every written communication with your landlord — texts, emails, notices.
  • Photograph any disputed condition (damage, needed repairs) with a timestamp.
  • Check your written lease for the specific clause the dispute relates to.
  • Most boards have strict filing deadlines after an incident — file as soon as you can.

Frequently Asked

Where do I file a complaint against a landlord in Ontario?

The Landlord and Tenant Board (LTB), at 1-888-332-3234.

Where do I file a complaint against a landlord in British Columbia?

The Residential Tenancy Branch (RTB), at 604-660-1020.

Where do I file a complaint against a landlord in Alberta?

The Residential Tenancy Dispute Resolution Service (RTDRS), toll-free at 310-0000 then 780-644-3000.

Where do I file a complaint against a landlord in Quebec?

The Tribunal administratif du logement (TAL), at 1-800-683-2245 outside Montréal/Laval/Longueuil.

What is ComplaintCA?

A Canadian civic complaint platform that routes you to the correct authority, with AI-assisted drafting in 7 languages, an MP finder, a lawyer panel, and cyber crime reporting support.

I'm Deaf or hard of hearing — how do I call a phone-only authority?

Use Canada VRS (free sign-language video relay, available 24/7) or dial 711 for a text-based relay operator — both let you place a real phone call to any number listed on this page.
