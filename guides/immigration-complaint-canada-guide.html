Delayed Immigration Application? What Actually Works

Your work permit, PR application, or citizenship case is stuck past the posted processing time. Unlike a bank or airline complaint, there's no single free ombudsman that will pick this up for you — the real path is different, and worth understanding before you spend money on anything.

Be aware: Parliament has discussed creating an independent IRCC Ombudsperson office, but as of this writing it does not exist. IRCC's own inventories currently show roughly 2.1 million applications in process, with about 922,700 of those past the service standard — so a delay, while frustrating, is common. This guide covers the escalation paths that actually exist today.

The Real Escalation Order

1IRCC Case Specific Enquiry

The official first step once you're past the posted processing time. It creates a documented record of your inquiry, even if the reply is often generic.

IRCC Web Form / Case Specific Enquiry →

2Contact your Member of Parliament

Your MP's office can submit a constituent inquiry to IRCC through its dedicated MP/Senator liaison channel. It doesn't guarantee faster processing, but it's a real, official escalation — and one of the most commonly used ones by Canadians in this exact situation.

ComplaintCA's MP finder helps you identify and reach your MP for this directly.

3Federal Court mandamus (last resort)

A court order compelling IRCC to make a decision — not to approve it, just to decide. Courts expect you to show the webform and MP steps first, and this requires a lawyer.

ComplaintCA's Lawyer Panel can connect you with counsel for this step.

Different problem: a bad immigration consultant

If the issue is your immigration consultant's conduct — not IRCC processing — it goes to their regulator, not IRCC.

Frequently Asked

Is there an ombudsman for IRCC?

No — unlike telecom, banking, or airlines, there's no independent ombudsperson office for immigration yet.

My application is past the posted time — what first?

Submit a Case Specific Enquiry through IRCC's official web form.

Can my MP actually help?

Yes — their office can submit a constituent inquiry through IRCC's MP liaison channel. No guarantee, but a real, documented escalation.

What is a writ of mandamus?

A Federal Court order compelling IRCC to make a decision on an unreasonably delayed file. Requires a lawyer and prior escalation attempts.

Where do I complain about my immigration consultant?

The College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC) — not IRCC.
